 Supermarket Sales Data Analysis

 Project Overview

This project analyzes supermarket sales data to understand key factors influencing revenue, customer behavior, and profitability across different branches.

The analysis focuses on identifying trends in sales performance, product demand, and customer segments to support data-driven business decisions.

---

 Objectives

- Identify the city and branch with highest revenue
- Analyze sales trends over time
- Evaluate product line performance
- Understand customer behavior (type & gender)
- Examine payment methods and spending patterns
- Explore factors affecting profitability

---

 Tools & Technologies

- Python
- Pandas
- Matplotlib
- Seaborn
- Google Colab

---

 Dataset

- Source: Supermarket sales dataset
- Records: 1000 rows
- Features: 17 columns including:
  - Branch, City, Customer Type
  - Product Line, Unit Price, Quantity
  - Total, Gross Income, Rating

---

 Data Cleaning & Preparation

- Checked and confirmed no missing values
- Converted Date column to datetime format
- Extracted new features:
  - Month
  - Day
- Verified data types and structure

---

 Key Analysis & Insights

 Revenue by City

- Naypyitaw generates the highest revenue
- Followed by Yangon and Mandalay

 Recommendation: Focus marketing and expansion strategies in Naypyitaw

---

 Branch Performance

- Branch C has the highest sales and profit

 Recommendation: Analyze and replicate strategies used in Branch C

---

 Sales Trends

- Sales fluctuate daily with no consistent pattern

 Recommendation: Introduce promotions during low-sales periods

---

 Product Performance

- Electronic accessories have the highest sales volume
- Food and beverages generate the highest profit

 Insight: High sales ≠ high profit

---

👥 Customer Behavior

- Members spend more than normal customers
- Female customers spend slightly more than males

 Recommendation: Strengthen loyalty programs and targeted marketing

---

 Payment Methods

- Most used: E-wallet and Cash

 Recommendation: Promote digital payment incentives

---

 Ratings & Satisfaction

- Ratings vary slightly across branches
- Weak relationship between rating and sales

---

 Profit Analysis

- Profit is highest in Branch C
- Influenced by:
  - Unit price
  - Quantity
  - Tax and cost (COGS)

---

 Key Findings

- Location significantly impacts revenue
- Customer type influences spending behavior
- Product strategy should balance volume and profit
- Payment preferences affect customer experience
